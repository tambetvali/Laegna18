# Laegna18 — Digital Bands, Linear–Exponential Flip, and U/V Symmetry

This article shows how:

- Laegna’s **digital system** integrates **linear** and **exponential** scales in separate bands.  
- Exponent becomes **locally linear** at each band center (e.g. 1, 2 at different octaves).  
- U and V define **inwards–outwards symmetry** of operations.  
- All symmetries reduce to **simple 1/2 and 1/4 relations**, not irrationals.

---

## 1. Digital bands: linear and exponential scales

Laegna treats numbers in **bands**:

- Digitwise: positions like I, O, A, E  
- Numberwise: scales like 1, 2, 4, 8, 16

Each band has:

- A **linear scale** (additive, plus/minus)  
- An **exponential scale** (multiplicative, power-based)

Example:

- Linear band around 1:  
  values like 0.5, 1, 2  
- Exponential band around 2:  
  values like $2^0 = 1$, $2^1 = 2$, $2^2 = 4$

At each band center, exponent and linear **align**:

- At 1:  
  $1^2 = 1$ and $2^0 = 1$  
- At 2:  
  $2^1 = 2$ and linear step from 1 to 2 is also 1 unit

Thus:

- Exponent becomes **locally linear** at band centers.  
- Laegna18 uses these centers as **exact breaking points**.

---

## 2. Exact flip at 1 and 2

Consider:

- Quadratic: $q(x) = x^2$  
- Exponential: $f(x) = 2^x$

Around 1:

- $q(1) = 1$  
- $f(0) = 1$

Around 2:

- $q(2) = 4$  
- $f(2) = 4$

Interpretation:

- At 1 and 2, **linear steps** and **exponential steps** produce the same values.  
- These are **flip points** where exponent can be treated as linear in that band.

Laegna18 uses:

- 1 as a **local symmetry center** for small scales  
- 2 as a **local symmetry center** for the next octave

---

## 3. U as internal low-band zero

U represents:

- **Internal, low-band zero**  
- External limit for multiplication and division

Meaning:

- Multiplication and division cannot pass U inward.  
- U is the **inwards boundary** of scaling.  
- Plus and minus cannot pass the **external center** outward.

Interpretation:

- Addition/subtraction stay within a band.  
- Multiplication/division at certain powers **collapse outwards** to the next band.  
- U is the point where inward scaling stops.

Example:

- Repeated addition of 1 never reaches the next octave by itself.  
- Repeated multiplication by 2 quickly reaches 2, 4, 8, 16.  
- U marks where inward scaling is saturated.

---

## 4. V as new cyclic beginning

V represents:

- **New cyclic beginning** of a band  
- Often a **shift fractal** of values in repetition

Interpretation:

- V is where a new **epoch of growth** starts.  
- Linearizing epochs of evolution:  
  each epoch is mapped to a band, with V as its start.

Digits accumulate:

- Moments of growth are counted as digits.  
- When their sum reaches a breakpoint (e.g. 2, 4, 8), a **flip** occurs.  
- This is the move from Z to X to Y in Laegna terms:  
  successive axes of representation.

---

## 5. Exponent mapping from out to in (V to U)

Exponent maps:

- From **outer bands** (large scales, V)  
- To **inner bands** (small scales, U)

This mapping is:

- Resonant  
- Synchronous  
- Symmetric with linear mapping from U outwards

Example:

- Exponential: $2^x$  
- Linear: $x$

From U outwards:

- Linear: $0 \to 1 \to 2 \to 4$  
- Exponential: $2^0 = 1$, $2^1 = 2$, $2^2 = 4$

From V inwards (normalized):

- Take large exponent, e.g. $2^8 = 256$  
- Normalize by band unit, e.g. divide by 16:  
  $256 / 16 = 16$  
- Now 16 behaves like a **local band center** again.

Thus:

- Exponent from V collapses inward to U-like centers.  
- Linear from U expands outward to V-like scales.  
- Symmetry is maintained.

## 6. Symmetries via 1/2 and 1/4

Laegna avoids irrationals by using:

- $1/2$ relations  
- $1/4$ relations

Examples:

- Half-scale:  
  $2 \to 1$ (divide by 2)

  $4 \to 1$ (divide by 4)

These relations:

- Define **inwards–outwards symmetry**.  
- Map exponent and linear behavior without irrational distances.

Interpretation:

- Exponent from outer bands is reduced by $1/2$ or $1/4$ to reach inner bands.  
- Linear from inner bands is multiplied by 2 or 4 to reach outer bands.  
- All symmetries are expressed in these simple fractions.

Classical curved space:

Laegna18:

This is how:

# Laegna18 — Linear Travel to Infinity and Symmetric Bands

This explains, in mathematical terms, how:

- The system travels **linearly from zero to infinity** at each precision.  
- Directional changes “at infinity” are reflected locally as **higher bands/channels**.  
- Infinity becomes a **simple two‑band model**: local half and infinite half, meeting in a symmetric point.

---

## 1. Linear travel from 0 to ∞ at each precision

Consider a **local step size** $h > 0$.

Linear travel:

$x_{n} = n h$

As $n \to \infty$, $x_{n} \to \infty$.

Now introduce a **band scaling factor** $k > 1$ (e.g. $k = 2$ or $4$).

Define:

- Local band: values in $[0, L]$  
- Higher band: values in $[L, kL]$

If we scale the step size in the higher band:

$h_{\text{band}} = k h$

Then:

- Local steps: $x_{n} = n h$  
- Band steps: $y_{n} = n h_{\text{band}} = n k h$

Interpretation:

- Band steps move $k$ times faster than local steps.  
- If $k = 2$, band travel is “two times faster” than local change.

In Laegna terms:

- Local precision: small $h$  
- Infinity‑relational precision: scaled $k h$  
- Balanced yin/yang: both are used together, one for short term, one for long term.

---

## 2. Balanced local and infinity steps

We define:

- Local step: $h$  
- Infinity‑related step: $H = 2h$

Take identical **count** of steps in both:

- Local: $x_{n} = n h$  
- Infinity band: $y_{n} = n H = 2 n h$

Then:

- For the same $n$, $y_{n} = 2 x_{n}$  
- Infinity band travels twice as fast.

Balanced yin/yang:

- Use $h$ for local detail.  
- Use $2h$ for long‑term band movement.  
- Together, they form a **two‑speed system** that is still linear.

---

## 3. Infinity as two halves: scope and repetition

We model infinity as:

- **Scope half**: large‑scale band  
- **Repetition half**: local repeated steps

Let total “size” be normalized to 1.

- Local half: size $1/2$  
- Infinity half: size $1/2$

Interpretation:

- Local half is infinitesimal in terms of **repetition**:  
  many small steps fill its half.  
- Infinity half is infinitesimal in terms of **scope**:  
  one large band represents vast range.

Mathematically:

- Local: $x_{n} = n h$, with $n$ large, $h$ small.  
- Infinity band: $Y = K$, a single large value representing the band.

Both occupy “half” of the conceptual space:

- Sum of local steps approximates $1/2$.  
- Single band value represents the other $1/2$.

---

## 4. Meeting point: symmetric infinity

We define a **symmetric point** where:

- Local repetition and infinity scope match in scale.

Let:

- Local accumulated value: $S = \sum_{n=1}^{N} h$  
- Infinity band value: $Y = K$

Choose $N$ and $h$ such that:

$S = K$

Then:

- Local half (repetition) and infinity half (scope) meet at the same magnitude.  
- This is the **symmetric point**.

In Laegna terms:

- Two bands of same scale:  
  one built by repetition, one by scope.  
- At the symmetric point, they are **indistinguishable in value**.

---

## 5. Decimals solving into same system

Local band:

- Uses decimals like $0.1,\;0.01,\;0.001$  
- These are small $h$ values.

Infinity band:

- Uses scaled units like $1,\;2,\;4,\;8$  
- These are large $K$ values.

By choosing:

- $N$ large enough  
- $h$ small enough  
- $K$ appropriate

We can make:

$\sum_{n=1}^{N} h = K$

Thus:

- Local decimals (repetition) and band units (scope) solve into the **same system**.  
- Two bands of same scale are created:  
  one detailed, one coarse.  
- Planning becomes precise: one can choose which band to use for which purpose.

---

## 6. Final interpretation in Laegna18

- Linear travel from 0 to ∞ is always possible at any precision $h$.  
- Higher bands/channels are just scaled versions (e.g. $2h$, $4h$).  
- Directional changes “at infinity” are reflected locally by switching bands.  
- Infinity is modeled as two halves:

  - Local half: repetition  
  - Infinity half: scope

- At the symmetric point, both halves match in value.  
- Decimals and bands belong to the same discrete, planable system.

This is how Laegna18 turns infinity into a simple, symmetric, two‑band model.
