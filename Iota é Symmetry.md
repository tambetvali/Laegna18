# Laegna18 — Digital Bands, Linear–Exponential Flip, and U/V Symmetry

This article shows how:

- Laegna’s **digital system** integrates **linear** and **exponential** scales in separate bands.  
- Exponent becomes **locally linear** at each band center (e.g. 1, 2 at different octaves).  
- U and V define **inwards–outwards symmetry** of operations.  
- All symmetries reduce to **simple 1/2 and 1/4 relations**, not irrationals.

---

## 1. Digital bands: linear and exponential scales

Laegna treats numbers in **bands**:

- Digitwise: positions like I, O, A, E  
- Numberwise: scales like 1, 2, 4, 8, 16

Each band has:

- A **linear scale** (additive, plus/minus)  
- An **exponential scale** (multiplicative, power-based)

Example:

- Linear band around 1:  
  values like 0.5, 1, 2  
- Exponential band around 2:  
  values like $2^0 = 1$, $2^1 = 2$, $2^2 = 4$

At each band center, exponent and linear **align**:

- At 1:  
  $1^2 = 1$ and $2^0 = 1$  
- At 2:  
  $2^1 = 2$ and linear step from 1 to 2 is also 1 unit

Thus:

- Exponent becomes **locally linear** at band centers.  
- Laegna18 uses these centers as **exact breaking points**.

---

## 2. Exact flip at 1 and 2

Consider:

- Quadratic: $q(x) = x^2$  
- Exponential: $f(x) = 2^x$

Around 1:

- $q(1) = 1$  
- $f(0) = 1$

Around 2:

- $q(2) = 4$  
- $f(2) = 4$

Interpretation:

- At 1 and 2, **linear steps** and **exponential steps** produce the same values.  
- These are **flip points** where exponent can be treated as linear in that band.

Laegna18 uses:

- 1 as a **local symmetry center** for small scales  
- 2 as a **local symmetry center** for the next octave

## 3. U as internal low-band zero

U represents:

- **Internal, low-band zero**  
- External limit for multiplication and division

Meaning:

- Multiplication and division cannot pass U inward.  
- U is the **inwards boundary** of scaling.  
- Plus and minus cannot pass the **external center** outward.

Interpretation:

- Addition/subtraction stay within a band.  
- Multiplication/division at certain powers **collapse outwards** to the next band.  
- U is the point where inward scaling stops.

Example:

- Repeated addition of 1 never reaches the next octave by itself.  
- Repeated multiplication by 2 quickly reaches 2, 4, 8, 16.  
- U marks where inward scaling is saturated.

---

## 4. V as new cyclic beginning

V represents:

- **New cyclic beginning** of a band  
- Often a **shift fractal** of values in repetition

Interpretation:

- V is where a new **epoch of growth** starts.  
- Linearizing epochs of evolution:  
  each epoch is mapped to a band, with V as its start.

Digits accumulate:

- Moments of growth are counted as digits.  
- When their sum reaches a breakpoint (e.g. 2, 4, 8), a **flip** occurs.  
- This is the move from Z to X to Y in Laegna terms:  
  successive axes of representation.

---

## 5. Exponent mapping from out to in (V to U)

Exponent maps:

- From **outer bands** (large scales, V)  
- To **inner bands** (small scales, U)

This mapping is:

- Resonant  
- Synchronous  
- Symmetric with linear mapping from U outwards

Example:

- Exponential: $2^x$  
- Linear: $x$

From U outwards:

- Linear: $0 \to 1 \to 2 \to 4$  
- Exponential: $2^0 = 1$, $2^1 = 2$, $2^2 = 4$

From V inwards (normalized):

- Take large exponent, e.g. $2^8 = 256$  
- Normalize by band unit, e.g. divide by 16:  
  $256 / 16 = 16$  
- Now 16 behaves like a **local band center** again.

Thus:

- Exponent from V collapses inward to U-like centers.  
- Linear from U expands outward to V-like scales.  
- Symmetry is maintained.

## 6. Symmetries via 1/2 and 1/4

Laegna avoids irrationals by using:

- $1/2$ relations  
- $1/4$ relations

Examples:

- Half-scale:  
  $2 \to 1$ (divide by 2)  
  $4 \to 2$ (divide by 2)  
- Quarter-scale:  
  $4 \to 1$ (divide by 4)  
  $8 \to 2$ (divide by 4)

These relations:

- Define **inwards–outwards symmetry**.  
- Map exponent and linear behavior without irrational distances.

Interpretation:

- Exponent from outer bands is reduced by $1/2$ or $1/4$ to reach inner bands.  
- Linear from inner bands is multiplied by 2 or 4 to reach outer bands.  
- All symmetries are expressed in these simple fractions.

## 7. From irrational curves to discrete symmetry

Classical curved space:

- Uses irrational lengths and continuous curves.  

Laegna18:

- Uses band centers (1, 2, 4, 8, 16).  
- Uses $1/2$ and $1/4$ to move between bands.  
- Treats exponent as locally linear at each band center.

Thus:

- Curved, irrational behavior is **measured symmetrically** to a square-like grid.  
- Not by following irrational curves, but by:

  - Anchoring at band centers  
  - Using simple fractional relations  
  - Collapsing exponent into local linear behavior

This is how:

- Laegna digital system integrates linear and exponential scales.  
- U and V define inwards–outwards limits.  
- Symmetries are expressed through $1/2$ and $1/4$, not irrationals.
